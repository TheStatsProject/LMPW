## Analysis

### Labor Force Contributions

Mexican migrant workers have become an integral part of the American workforce, contributing significantly to:

- **Agriculture**: Essential labor force in farming and food production
- **Construction**: Key contributors to the building and infrastructure sectors
- **Service Industry**: Critical workforce in hospitality, healthcare, and retail

### Economic Output

The GDP contribution of Mexican migrant labor demonstrates their substantial role in the U.S. economy. Their participation spans across multiple industries, generating significant economic output and supporting business growth.

### Tax Revenue

Contrary to common misconceptions, Mexican migrant workers contribute billions in tax revenue annually, supporting state and local government services and infrastructure.

## Conclusion

The data clearly indicates that Mexican migrant labor represents a vital component of the U.S. economy, contributing positively to GDP, tax revenue, employment, and consumer spending.
